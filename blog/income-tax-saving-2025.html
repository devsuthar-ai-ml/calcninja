Income Tax Saving 2024-25: Deductions & Tips

Published on January 13, 2025 | 8 min read

Old vs New Tax Regime: Which is Better?

In 2024-25, India offers two tax regimes. Most salaried employees pay 15-30% less tax under the old regime due to deductions. Let's compare:

IncomeNew Regime TaxOld Regime (with 80C)Savings
₹5,00,000₹0₹0₹0
₹10,00,000₹42,500₹6,250₹36,250
₹20,00,000₹3,12,500₹2,12,500₹1,00,000

For most people: Old regime saves ₹30,000-₹2,00,000 per year

Section 80C: Save up to ₹1,50,000

Under Section 80C, these investments are 100% tax-deductible:

  • Life Insurance Premium: Buy a term plan (₹1L/month = ₹12L/year deductible)
  • PPF (Public Provident Fund): Contribute up to ₹1,50,000/year
  • ELSS (Equity Linked Saving Scheme): Mutual funds with 3-year lock-in
  • Home Loan Principal: Principal portion of EMI is eligible
  • Fixed Deposits (5-year): Bank/post office 5-year FDs
  • NSC (National Saving Certificate): Post office scheme
  • Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana: For girl child education
  • ULIPs (Unit Linked Insurance Plans): Insurance + investment combined

Example: Save ₹1,50,000 in PPF = ₹39,000 tax saved (at 26% slab)

Section 80D: Medical Insurance Deduction

Get deduction for health insurance premiums:

  • Yourself + Family: Up to ₹25,000 deduction
  • Parents (age 60+): Up to ₹25,000 additional
  • Senior Citizen (age 60+): Up to ₹50,000 deduction

Example: Annual health insurance premium ₹25,000 = ₹6,500 tax saved (26% slab)

Other Important Deductions

Section 80TTA (Savings Account Interest)
Interest on savings account up to ₹10,000 is deductible (for non-senior citizens)

Section 80E (Education Loan Interest)
Interest on education loans is 100% deductible (no limit!)

Section 80G (Charity Donations)
Donations to registered NGOs are 50% deductible

House Rent Allowance (HRA)
If paying rent, claim HRA (no tax on 40-50% of salary)

Step-by-Step Tax Saving Plan

Month 1-2: Insurance (₹50,000)

  • Buy a term life insurance policy = ₹13,000 tax saved

Month 3-6: PPF (₹50,000)

  • Invest in PPF = ₹13,000 tax saved
  • Gets 7.1% interest (completely tax-free!)

Month 7-9: Home Loan (Auto-deducted)

  • If taking home loan, principal amount is auto-deductible
  • Example: ₹25L loan at 8% for 20 years = ₹8,500 tax saved first year

Month 10-12: ELSS Fund (₹50,000)

  • Invest in tax-saving mutual fund (3-year lock-in)
  • Potential 12-15% growth = ₹13,000 tax saving + ₹6,000-₹7,500 profit

Total Tax Saving in 1 Year: ₹39,500+ (Net cost of investments: ₹1,10,500 only)

Pro Tips to Save More Tax

  1. Max Out 80C: Reach ₹1,50,000 limit every year
  2. Don't Keep Excess Salary: Excess cash encourages spending, not saving
  3. Use HRA Effectively: If eligible, don't waste it
  4. Invest in PPF: Returns are tax-free, perfect for 10-15 year goals
  5. Take Health Insurance: 80D is easiest deduction (just buy a policy)
  6. Understand Your Slab: At 26% slab, ₹1L investment saves ₹26K tax
  7. File on Time: File ITR by July 31 to claim deductions

FAQs

Q: Is old regime always better?
A: For most people yes. But for high earners (₹50L+), new regime may be better.

Q: Can I invest in 80C after year ends?
A: No. But you can invest in Jan-Mar and claim in current year ITR.

Q: What if I don't have ₹1,50,000 to invest?
A: Even ₹50,000 investment saves ₹13,000 tax. Every rupee counts!
