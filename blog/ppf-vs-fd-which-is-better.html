PPF vs FD: Which is Better for Saving?

Published on January 8, 2025 | 6 min read

PPF Definition

PPF (Public Provident Fund) is a government-backed long-term savings scheme with guaranteed returns and zero tax.

FD Definition

FD (Fixed Deposit) is a bank deposit where you lend money at fixed interest for a fixed tenure.

Initial amount: ₹1,00,000

Net after tax: ₹1,98,742

Difference: PPF gives ₹81,501 more after tax! 🎯

Why PPF Wins Over FD

1. Tax-Free Returns

  • PPF interest = 0% tax (completely tax-free)
  • FD interest = 30% TDS (massive loss)
  • At 26% income slab, difference is ₹4.2% on FD every year

2. 80C Deduction

  • PPF investment = Tax deduction under 80C
  • ₹1L PPF investment = ₹26,000 tax saved (26% slab)
  • FD = No deduction at all
  • Real benefit = 7.1% interest + ₹26K tax saving = 33% effective return Year 1!

3. Higher Returns

  • PPF 7.1% vs FD 6% = 1.1% difference (compounds hugely over 15 years)

When Should You Choose FD Over PPF?

1. Short-term Goal (< 7 years)

  • Need money in 2-3 years? PPF locks your money (partial withdrawal only after 7 years)
  • FD is perfect for immediate liquidity

2. Large One-Time Investment

  • PPF max = ₹1,50,000/year (spread over 2 years for ₹3L)
  • Got ₹5L windfall? Use FD to invest immediately

3. Below-Slab Income

  • If your income is below tax slab (e.g., retiree with <₹5L income)
  • FD interest = No TDS deducted (you already don't pay tax)
  • PPF advantage (80C deduction) doesn't help

Smart Strategy: Use Both!

PPF Withdrawal Rules

  • Years 1-6: No withdrawal allowed (pure lock-in)
  • Years 7+: Can withdraw up to 50% of balance or previous year balance (whichever is less)
  • Year 15+: Complete withdrawal allowed (investment ends)
  • Year 15+: Can extend for 5-year blocks with same rules

How to Open PPF Account?

  1. Walk into any bank branch (SBI, HDFC, ICICI, etc.)
  2. Ask for PPF account form
  3. Fill form with: Name, PAN, Aadhar, DOB
  4. Submit with: PAN copy, ID copy, proof of address
  5. Complete within 15 days
  6. Start paying ₹500+ every financial year

OR

Open online via SBI, HDFC, ICICI banking apps!

How to Open FD Account?

  1. Visit bank branch or open in their app
  2. Choose tenure (1 month to 10 years)
  3. Choose amount
  4. Choose compounding frequency
  5. Approve via OTP
  6. Money locked, auto-maturity date set

Inflation Impact

Real concern: After inflation, your savings might lose purchasing power

  • Inflation rate in India: 2-6% per annum
  • PPF return (7.1%): Beats inflation by 1.1-5.1% (real return)
  • FD return (6%): Barely beats inflation (real return 0-4%)
  • Winner: PPF

The Verdict: PPF vs FD

✅ Choose PPF if:

  • You have 15-year investment horizon
  • You want tax-free growth
  • You earn taxable income (₹5L+)
  • You can commit to regular investment

✅ Choose FD if:

  • You need money within 5-7 years
  • You have one-time large amount
  • You want complete liquidity
  • Immediate emergency fund

FAQs

Q: Can I open multiple PPF accounts?
A: No. One person can have only ONE PPF account (per PAN).

Q: What happens to PPF after 15 years?
A: You must withdraw or extend in 5-year blocks at same or new rate.

Q: Is FD interest completely taxed?
A: Interest is taxed at your slab rate. Banks deduct 30% TDS if interest >₹40K/year.
