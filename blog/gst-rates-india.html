GST Rates in India: Complete Category Breakdown

Published on January 12, 2025 | 6 min read

What is GST?

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a single tax on production of goods and services. It applies to most transactions in India with 4 tax slabs: 0%, 5%, 12%, and 18%. CGST (Central) + SGST (State) = Total GST.

The 4 GST Slabs

SlabRateCGSTSGST
0% (Exempted)0%0%0%
5% (Essential)5%2.5%2.5%
12% (Standard)12%6%6%
18% (Luxury)18%9%9%

0% GST Products (Exempted)

  • Food Items: Vegetables, fruits, grains, salt, eggs, milk
  • Medicines: Most prescription medicines
  • Newspapers & Books: Educational books (not notebooks)
  • Services: Public transportation, education, health services
  • Banking: Interest on loans

Example: ₹100 vegetables = ₹100 (no GST added)

5% GST Products (Essential Goods)

  • Food & Beverages: Packaged food, tea, coffee, butter
  • Toiletries: Toothpaste, soap
  • Clothing: Basic wear (not branded/designer)
  • Oil & Fuel: Cooking oil, coal
  • Transport: Local transport (auto, taxi)

Example: ₹100 oil = ₹100 + ₹5 GST = ₹105

12% GST Products (Standard Rate)

  • Home Appliances: Washing machine, refrigerator, microwave
  • Electronics: Computers, laptops, keyboards
  • Furniture: Tables, chairs, sofas
  • Construction: Bricks, cement, steel
  • Services: Restaurant meals, courier, dry cleaning
  • Cosmetics: Fancy cosmetics

Example: ₹10,000 refrigerator = ₹10,000 + ₹1,200 GST = ₹11,200

18% GST Products (Luxury)

  • Premium Electronics: Cameras, projectors, phones (premium)
  • Vehicles: Cars, motorcycles, scooters
  • Jewelry: Branded watches, designer jewelry
  • Premium Services: Hotels, air travel, restaurants (5-star)
  • AC Equipment: Air conditioners
  • Gaming: Video games, gaming consoles

Example: ₹50,000 phone = ₹50,000 + ₹9,000 GST = ₹59,000

Quick GST Calculation

To Add GST: Price × (1 + GST Rate / 100)

  • ₹1,000 product at 18% = ₹1,000 × 1.18 = ₹1,180
  • ₹1,000 product at 12% = ₹1,000 × 1.12 = ₹1,120
  • ₹1,000 product at 5% = ₹1,000 × 1.05 = ₹1,050

To Remove GST: Price / (1 + GST Rate / 100)

  • ₹1,180 (including 18% GST) ÷ 1.18 = ₹1,000
  • ₹1,120 (including 12% GST) ÷ 1.12 = ₹1,000

Common Misconceptions

❌ Myth: GST is always 18%
✅ Fact: GST varies from 0% to 18% based on product type

❌ Myth: I pay full GST amount
✅ Fact: GST is split between Center (CGST) and State (SGST)

❌ Myth: All food has 0% GST
✅ Fact: Only raw food has 0%. Packaged food is 5% or 12%

❌ Myth: GST increases product cost
✅ Fact: GST is neutral. Businesses claim input GST credit.

GST Rate Changes (Recent Updates)

  • Jan 2024: Hotel rooms above ₹7,500/night charged 5% GST (down from 12%)
  • 2023: Renewable solar equipment reduced to 5% GST
  • Ongoing: Government keeps reviewing rates for essential items

For Business Owners

When to Register for GST?
Turnover exceeds ₹40 lakhs (₹20L for states like Hp, NE)

Input Tax Credit:
Buy goods at 12% GST, sell at 18%, you pay only 6% GST (difference)

File Every 3 Months:
GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B returns on GST portal

FAQs

Q: Does GST apply to everything?
A: No. Services like education, insurance, banking are often exempted.

Q: Can I negotiate GST?
A: No. It's a fixed government tax.

Q: Do I get bill with separate GST?
A: Yes, good businesses show CGST and SGST separately on bills.
